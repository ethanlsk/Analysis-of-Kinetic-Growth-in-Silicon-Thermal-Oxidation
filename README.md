# Analysis of Kinetic Growth in Silicon Thermal Oxidation
Semiconductor Experimentation Research Project

## Overview
This repository contains a semiconductor fabrication research project focused on the **thermal oxidation of silicon wafers** and the kinetic growth behavior of silicon dioxide (**SiO₂**) thin films. The project combined **hands-on wafer processing, simulation modeling, and surface characterization** to study how oxidation temperature and time affect oxide thickness, growth rate, and material properties.

This work demonstrates key principles used in **microelectronics manufacturing, transistor fabrication, and semiconductor process engineering**.

---

## Project Objectives

- Grow a controlled SiO₂ insulating layer on silicon wafers using thermal oxidation
- Analyze oxide growth using the **Deal-Grove Model**
- Evaluate activation energy through **Arrhenius analysis**
- Compare experimental and simulated oxidation results
- Characterize the wafer surface using **Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)**
- Investigate nanowire formations after oxidation

---

## Why Silicon Dioxide Matters

Silicon dioxide is one of the most critical materials in semiconductor technology.

It is commonly used as:

- Gate oxide in MOSFET transistors
- Electrical insulator between conductive regions
- Surface passivation layer
- Thin-film dielectric in integrated circuits

Accurate oxide thickness control is essential for modern chip scaling and performance.

---

## Experimental Process

### Thermal Oxidation of Silicon Wafer

A silicon wafer was oxidized using a high-temperature furnace under controlled atmospheric conditions.

### Process Parameters

- Wafer Diameter: **100 mm**
- Oxidation Temperature: **975°C**
- Oxidation Duration: **20 hours**
- Controlled oxygen environment
- Gradual cooldown under inert atmosphere

This process formed a silicon dioxide layer directly on the wafer surface.

---

## Simulation & Modeling

### nanoHUB Process Lab: Oxidation

The oxidation process was modeled using nanoHUB software based on the **Deal-Grove linear-parabolic oxidation model**.

Simulation temperatures included:

- 850°C
- 975°C
- 1100°C
- 1225°C

Outputs studied:

- Oxide thickness vs time
- Temperature dependence
- Growth rate trends

### Arrhenius Plot Analysis

Arrhenius plots were used to estimate activation energy and understand the reaction kinetics controlling oxide growth.

---

## Experimental Results

- Successful oxidation of silicon wafer
- Visible oxide coloration indicating film growth
- Estimated oxide thickness of approximately **200–300 nm**
- Strong temperature dependence observed in simulations
- Growth trends aligned with Deal-Grove theory

---

## Surface Characterization

### Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)

SEM imaging confirmed:

- Presence of silicon and oxygen on the surface
- Formation of silicon dioxide layer
- Nanowire structures present after oxidation

These nanowires may have future applications in nanoelectronics and advanced sensing devices.

---

## Key Findings

- Thermal oxidation is effective for producing high-quality SiO₂ layers
- Oxide growth can be predicted using established semiconductor models
- Arrhenius analysis provides insight into activation energy behavior
- Surface nanostructures formed unexpectedly and may warrant future study
